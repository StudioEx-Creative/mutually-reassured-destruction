Chapter 02 · The Map

It is not happening evenly. But it is happening everywhere.

Warming is not uniform: land heats faster than ocean, and the Arctic faster than anywhere. The band below shows how far each latitude has departed from its 1951–1980 normal. The live map of hazards near you waits in the final chapter.

Step 02 · Your area

What's happening near you

With your permission, this shows recent hazards around you — fires, quakes, severe weather — and how today's temperature compares with your local normal. Your coordinates are used only to query public hazard APIs as a bounding box, and are never sent anywhere else.

Opt-in only. Polling pauses when this tab is hidden. Coordinates never leave your browser except as bounding-box queries to the hazard providers themselves (GDACS, NASA FIRMS, USGS, Open-Meteo, OpenAQ). See the privacy policy.

Methodology & disclaimer ▾
A work of data art and advocacy, not a policy document. The climate is a system of staggering complexity, and every projection here carries uncertainty that we have tried to surface, not hide. The point is not the precision of any single number; it is the direction of all of them taken together. We have not invented or inflated any figure; all data was verified against primary sources in June 2026. Where authoritative sources disagree, we use the most recent peer-reviewed value and note the disagreement. Full sources, retrieval dates and refresh notes are in the project's DATA-SOURCES file.
The world is not simple.
But the arithmetic of carbon is, and so is the choice it leaves us.
